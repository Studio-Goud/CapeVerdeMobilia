# Djarvista — Brand Strategy

> **Status:** Brand strategy document, v0.1 · **Date:** 2026-07-20
> **Classification legend:** **FACT** (confirmed source) · **ASSUMPTION** (single/indirect source) · **HYPOTHESIS** (reasoned guess) · **RECOMMENDATION** (our advice)
> All external claims are cited to the project canon. Sources accessed **2026-07-20**. Name, domain, trademark and social-handle availability are **never guaranteed** and must be confirmed via official checks (see §7).

---

## 1. Purpose of this document

This document defines the brand foundations for the platform. It:

1. Explores a wide field of **candidate names** (§3) against explicit **criteria** (§2).
2. Shortlists the **best five** and profiles each (§4).
3. Confirms **Djarvista** as the recommended name (consistent with the project canon) and develops the full brand system — mission, vision, values, promise, tone of voice, tagline, pitches and descriptions (§5–§6).
4. Flags **availability items that must be validated officially** (§7).

The brand must serve a platform that is deliberately **not "just a listings site"** but **independent digital infrastructure** connecting citizens, professionals, investors and public bodies for property, building and public information in Cabo Verde. (**RECOMMENDATION**, per canon positioning.)

## 2. Naming criteria

Every candidate is scored against the following. These are the criteria the canon and brief require.

| # | Criterion | Why it matters |
|---|-----------|----------------|
| C1 | **Internationally pronounceable** | Users span PT, Creole (kea), EN, NL, FR; diaspora and foreign buyers must say it easily. |
| C2 | **Fits Cabo Verde** | Should feel local/rooted, not a generic Silicon-Valley coinage parachuted in. |
| C3 | **Trustworthy / institutional** | Platform is trust-first infrastructure that works with public bodies; the name must not sound like a flippy classifieds app. |
| C4 | **Modern / tech-credible** | Must work as a digital product, app icon, wordmark. |
| C5 | **Scalable to other countries** | Ambition is export to comparable island states; name must not hard-bind to one island or one country. |
| C6 | **Not housing-only** | Covers property **and** building **and** public/government information — must not literally mean "houses". |
| C7 | **Suitable for property + build + government** | Must sit credibly next to a municipality or ministry, an architect, and a buyer alike. |
| C8 | **Domain potential** | A plausible path to a usable `.com` / `.cv` / brandable domain (to validate — never assume). |
| C9 | **No unwanted meaning** in PT / Creole / EN / NL / FR | Screen for embarrassing or negative connotations across the five relevant languages. |

**Scoring key used in §3:** ✓ = strong fit, ~ = partial/uncertain, ✗ = weak. All C9 checks are **preliminary desk screening only** and require native-speaker + legal confirmation.

## 4. Shortlist — the best 5

Selection favours names that clear **C6 (not housing-only)**, **C7 (property + build + government)** and **C5 (export)** while still feeling Cabo-Verdean and pronounceable. Shortlist: **Djarvista, Portada, Terrapraça, Praça, Chave.**

### 4.1 Djarvista *(recommended — see §5)*

- **Meaning:** *djar* (island, in Kabuverdianu/Creole) + *vista* (view) → “island view” — the archipelago and the outlook it offers.
- **Positioning:** Independent, trust-first digital infrastructure for property, building and public information.
- **Slogan:** "From parcel to place." (EN) · "Da terra à vila." (PT)
- **Pros:** Coined & ownable (strong trademark/domain potential, to validate); not housing-only; covers land + settlement + civic; travels internationally; soft, warm sound; the accented **í** gives a distinctive, memorable wordmark.
- **Cons:** Coined word needs meaning-building (no instant recognition); the accent may be dropped in typing/URLs (needs an ASCII fallback, e.g. `djarvista`); must confirm no clash across languages.
- **International scalability:** High. Meaning is not country-bound; works as `@djarvista` namespace across markets.
- **Brand feel:** Rooted, modern, quietly institutional — a trustworthy utility rather than a hype app.

### 4.2 Portada

- **Meaning:** From *porta/portal* — a gateway; matches the canon tagline "the digital gateway…".
- **Positioning:** The gateway/front door to property and public information.
- **Slogan:** "Your gateway to property in Cabo Verde."
- **Pros:** Direct fit with the "gateway" narrative; pronounceable; not housing-only; institutional.
- **Cons:** *Portada* also means "book cover / front page" in Spanish/Portuguese — a competing meaning that dilutes the intended one; likely crowded namespace.
- **International scalability:** Medium-high; concept of a "portal" travels but off-meaning follows.
- **Brand feel:** Functional, portal-like, slightly utilitarian.

### 4.3 Terrapraça

- **Meaning:** *terra* (land) + *praça* (town square) → "the public square for land".
- **Positioning:** A neutral civic marketplace where citizens, professionals and public bodies meet.
- **Slogan:** "The public square for property and building."
- **Pros:** Captures both the **marketplace** and the **civic/neutral** ideas; not housing-only; strong C7 fit.
- **Cons:** Compound and a little long; two strong roots can feel busy; harder as a short app label.
- **International scalability:** Medium-high (Romance-language legible; less so in NL/EN cold).
- **Brand feel:** Civic, communal, marketplace-forward.

### 4.4 Praça

- **Meaning:** The town square — the historic neutral meeting place of a Cabo-Verdean town.
- **Positioning:** The neutral public space, now digital.
- **Slogan:** "Meet at the square."
- **Pros:** Deeply Cabo-Verdean and warm; instantly meaningful; embodies neutrality/trust.
- **Cons:** A common noun → very hard to trademark or own a domain; low distinctiveness; SEO nightmare.
- **International scalability:** Medium; charming but not universally understood.
- **Brand feel:** Civic, familiar, human — but generic.

### 4.5 Chave

- **Meaning:** *Chave* = key — "the key to property, building and procedures".
- **Positioning:** The key that unlocks property and administrative processes.
- **Slogan:** "The key to property in Cabo Verde."
- **Pros:** Evocative, positive, easy; strong metaphor for access/unlocking.
- **Cons:** **Direct overlap with the national e-ID "Chave Móvel Digital (CMDCV)"** could confuse users or trigger a government/legal objection — a real conflict for a platform that partners with public bodies; common noun → weak distinctiveness.
- **International scalability:** Medium.
- **Brand feel:** Friendly, access-oriented — but risky given the state-service name clash.

**Conclusion:** Djarvista is the strongest all-round performer and is the canon's recommended name. It is the only shortlisted option that is simultaneously ownable, non-generic, not housing-only, export-ready, and free of a known state-service name clash (**RECOMMENDATION**; all availability still **to validate** — §7).

## 5. Recommended name: Djarvista

> **RECOMMENDATION (per project canon):** Adopt **Djarvista** as the brand name. Code namespace `djarvista`; packages `@djarvista/*`. Combines *djar* (island, in Kabuverdianu/Creole) + *vista* (view) — “island view”. Trademark, domain and social handles are **to validate via official checks** and are **not guaranteed** (§7).

### 5.1 Mission

To make property, building and public information in Cabo Verde **transparent, trustworthy and accessible to everyone** — citizens, professionals, investors and public bodies — through independent digital infrastructure.

### 5.2 Vision

A Cabo Verde where any person, at home or in the diaspora, can find reliable information, trusted professionals and clear procedures for land, building and public services from their phone — and a model proven on the islands that can be exported to comparable island states. (Ambition per canon: pilot on São Vicente → multi-island → national → export.)

### 5.3 Core values

1. **Trust first.** Verification and neutrality over volume. Paid visibility never buys verification, review scores, or manipulation of official information — and is always visibly labelled.
2. **Independence.** Neutral infrastructure, not an agency and not a party to deals.
3. **Transparency.** Sources are cited; official texts preserved; sponsored content labelled "Patrocinado/Sponsored".
4. **Accessibility & inclusion.** Mobile-first, multilingual (pt/kea/en/nl/fr), WCAG 2.1 AA, plain-language summaries alongside official text.
5. **Rootedness.** Built for Cabo Verde's reality, with local language and local partners — not a generic import.
6. **Accountability.** Clear provenance of information and clear escalation when something is wrong.

### 5.4 Brand promise

**"If it's on Djarvista, you can act on it."** Information you can trust, people you can verify, and steps you can actually follow.

### 5.5 Tone of voice

- **Clear, not clever.** Plain language first; jargon and legalese translated, never dumped.
- **Calm and credible.** Sounds like trustworthy infrastructure, not a hype startup.
- **Warm and human.** Cabo-Verdean warmth; concierge-minded ("we'll help you through it").
- **Honest about uncertainty.** Distinguishes verified facts from unverified claims; says "to validate" rather than overstating.
- **Bilingual by default,** respectful of official Portuguese and everyday Creole.
- **Do / Don't:** Do say "verified", "official source", "sponsored (labelled)". Don't imply government endorsement we don't have, don't guarantee outcomes, don't present availability or legal status as settled.

### 5.6 Tagline

- **EN:** "The digital gateway to property, building and public information in Cabo Verde."
- **PT:** "A porta digital para terra, construção e serviços em Cabo Verde."
- **Short brand line (RECOMMENDATION):** "From parcel to place." / "Da terra à vila."

### 5.7 Elevator pitch (30 seconds)

> Djarvista is independent digital infrastructure for property, building and public information in Cabo Verde. Today this market runs on personal networks and scattered, hard-to-trust information — foreign buyers can't navigate it, and even locals lose time and money on opaque procedures. Djarvista brings five things into one trusted, mobile-first place: reliable **information**, verifiable **trust**, **findability**, a path to **transactions and jobs**, and **step-by-step process guidance**. We're not "just a listings site" — we're the trust layer that connects citizens, professionals, investors and public bodies. We start with a pilot on São Vicente and scale across the islands, then to comparable island states.

### 5.8 Short company description (≈50 words)

> **Djarvista** is an independent, trust-first digital platform for property, building and public information in Cabo Verde. It connects citizens, professionals, investors and public bodies through verified profiles, reliable information and clear process guidance — mobile-first and multilingual. Djarvista is neutral infrastructure, not an agency. Pilot on São Vicente, then multi-island.

### 5.9 Longer company description (≈150 words)

> **Djarvista** is independent digital infrastructure connecting citizens, professionals, investors and public bodies around property, building and public information in Cabo Verde. The market today is fragmented, informal and network-dependent: information is scattered and hard to trust, foreign buyers face uncertainty, and administrative procedures are opaque. Djarvista addresses this with five value layers in one place — **information, trust, findability, transactions/jobs, and process guidance** — built on a tiered **verification system (L0–L5)** and human-assisted **concierge** operations.
>
> The platform is mobile-first (internet penetration ~73.5%, mobile connections ~115% of population — **FACT/high**, DataReportal 2025) and multilingual (Portuguese, Kabuverdianu, English, Dutch, French). It runs a **hybrid business model**: professional subscriptions, premium/featured listings (always labelled), verification and lead services, and phased government partnerships. Paid visibility never buys trust or manipulates official information. Djarvista pilots on São Vicente, expands multi-island and national, and is designed to export to comparable island states.

## 6. Visual & verbal identity direction (RECOMMENDATION)

*Direction only — design to be produced separately. Non-binding.*

- **Wordmark:** lowercase **djarvista** with a distinctive accent on the **í**; ASCII fallback `djarvista` for URLs, handles and code (namespace `djarvista`, packages `@djarvista/*`).
- **Feel:** calm, institutional-modern; a utility you trust, not an app that shouts.
- **Colour direction (HYPOTHESIS):** an island/ocean palette (deep blue = trust/water) with a warm earth/terracotta accent (land/*terra*) — final palette to be set by design + accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA contrast).
- **Verbal system:** trust badges (L0–L5), the "Patrocinado/Sponsored" label, and a consistent "source & provenance" line on information pages.
