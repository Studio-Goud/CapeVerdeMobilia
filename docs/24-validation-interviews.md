# Kavíla — Validation & Interview Plan

> **Status:** Research/validation plan, v0.1 · **Date:** 2026-07-20
> **Classification legend:** **FACT** · **ASSUMPTION** · **HYPOTHESIS** · **RECOMMENDATION**
> **Method note:** These are **behavioural** interview guides, not opinion polls. We do **not** ask "would you use this?" or "do you like the idea?". We probe the **last real transaction/process** the person went through and reconstruct what actually happened — what was hard, what errors occurred, time lost, who was trusted, costs incurred, what information was missing, and **what they already pay for**. Past behaviour predicts future behaviour; opinions about a hypothetical product do not.

---

## 1. How to run these interviews

**Principles**
1. **Anchor on a real, recent event.** "Walk me through the *last* time you [bought/sold/permitted/hired/registered]…". Reconstruct it step by step.
2. **Ask about the past, never the future.** Avoid "would you", "could you", "do you like". Ask "what did you do", "what happened", "what did it cost".
3. **Follow the pain and the money.** Where did they lose time, redo work, or pay someone? What did they already pay for (and how much)?
4. **Silence and "tell me more."** Let them narrate; probe specifics (names of steps, offices, documents, amounts, dates).
5. **Ask who they trusted** and why — trust is the core of Kavíla.
6. **Capture verbatim quotes** and hard numbers (time, money, number of visits/errors).
7. **No pitching until the end.** Do not describe Kavíla before the behavioural section; it biases answers.

**Logistics**
- 30–45 min, in pt/kea/en as appropriate; record with consent (data protection — **CNPD/legal verification required**).
- One interviewer + one note-taker where possible.
- Score each interview with the model in §4 immediately after.

**Universal probes (reusable across every guide)**
- "How long did that step take? How long did the whole thing take?"
- "What did it cost you — fees, professionals, travel, time off work?"
- "What went wrong or had to be redone? How did you find out?"
- "What information did you wish you'd had at the start? Where did you eventually get it?"
- "Who did you trust to help, and why them?"
- "What do you already pay for today to make this easier?"

## 2. Interview guides by segment

Each guide has 6–10 questions probing the **last real** transaction/process. Adapt wording to the person; keep the behavioural framing.

### 2.1 Municipalities (câmaras municipais)
1. Walk me through the **last** property/building request a citizen brought to your front office — what did they come in for?
2. What information did they arrive **without**, that they needed? How often does that happen?
3. What made that case slow or require repeat visits? Where did it get stuck?
4. What errors do you most often see in submitted requests, and how are they caught?
5. In what language(s) did that interaction happen? Any language friction?
6. What do citizens most often phone or come back to ask **after** leaving?
7. When you need current procedure information yourselves, where do you get it, and how confident are you it's up to date?
8. What already takes staff time that a good self-serve information page would have handled?

### 2.2 Ministries
1. Describe the **last** time your department published or updated procedure/eligibility information for the public — how did that happen?
2. How do citizens currently find that information, based on what reaches you?
3. What misunderstandings or wrong assumptions by citizens create the most rework for you?
4. How does your information flow to/through **gov.cv / Porton di nôs Ilha / NOSi** today?
5. What languages do you publish in, and where do gaps appear?
6. When information changes (e.g. the 2026 tax change), how do you push the update out, and how long until the public reflects it?
7. What would a trusted external channel need to guarantee for you to be comfortable it linking to your official texts?

### 2.3 Cadastre / INGT (land registration)
1. Walk me through the **last** registration case that was harder than usual — what happened?
2. Where do errors/inconsistencies most often enter (documents, boundaries, prior records)?
3. How long did that case take end to end, versus a clean one?
4. What information do applicants most often arrive missing?
5. How did **LMITS**/the modernised process change that case versus the old way? (**FACT/med** — LMITS cut months→weeks.)
6. Which other bodies (notary, Conservatória, municipality) did that case have to coordinate with, and where did handoffs slow it?
7. What do applicants misunderstand about registration that causes rework?

### 2.4 Real-estate agents (makelaars)
1. Walk me through your **last** completed transaction — from first contact to close.
2. Where did you lose the most time, and why?
3. How did the buyer find you? How do buyers usually find you today?
4. What do you currently pay for to get leads or visibility (portals, ads, referrals)? How much?
5. What information about a property or a buyer did you have to chase, and from where?
6. What made a buyer trust you (or hesitate)? Any verification they asked for?
7. What fell through in a recent deal, and what caused it?
8. How do foreign/diaspora buyers differ from local ones in what they need?

### 2.5 Architects
1. Walk me through your **last** project from client contact through permitting.
2. Where did the **permitting/municipal** steps slow you down? What was unclear?
3. How did that client find you?
4. What do you pay for today to be found or to win work?
5. What information about regulations/procedures did you have to hunt for, and where?
6. What errors in submissions caused resubmission or delay?
7. How do you demonstrate credentials/credibility to a new client?

### 2.6 Contractors (builders)
1. Walk me through your **last** build job — how you won it and how it ran.
2. How did the client find and choose you? What made them trust you?
3. What did you pay for to get that job or to advertise generally?
4. Where did delays or disputes come from (materials, permits, payments)?
5. What information (rules, costs, availability) was missing when you quoted?
6. How did you handle the paperwork/permits — who did what?
7. What went wrong on a recent job, and what did it cost you?

### 2.7 Tradespeople (electricians, plumbers, masons, etc.)
1. Walk me through your **last** paid job — how the customer found you.
2. How do most of your customers find you today (word of mouth, WhatsApp, other)?
3. Do you pay anything to get customers or advertise? How much?
4. What made that customer trust you enough to hire you?
5. Have you lost a job because a customer couldn't verify you were reliable? What happened?
6. When you're not busy, how do you fill the gap?
7. What's frustrating about getting paid / agreeing scope with new customers?

### 2.8 Landowners
1. Walk me through the **last** time you sold, rented, registered or tried to develop a piece of land.
2. What documents/proof did you need, and which were hard to get?
3. Who did you rely on to help (agent, lawyer, notary, family), and why them?
4. What did the whole thing cost you — fees and time?
5. What was unclear about ownership, boundaries or registration?
6. Did anything go wrong or get delayed? What caused it?
7. How did a buyer/tenant find you, or you them?

### 2.9 Foreign buyers
1. Walk me through your **last** (or attempted) property purchase in Cabo Verde, from search to now.
2. How did you search, and what information did you struggle to find or trust?
3. Who did you rely on locally, and how did you decide to trust them?
4. What did you (or would you) pay for — lawyer, agent, translation, due diligence? Roughly how much?
5. What confused or worried you about ownership rights, tax or the registration/notary process?
6. What nearly made you walk away, or did?
7. What did you have to do remotely vs on-island, and what was hardest from abroad?
8. What language did everything happen in, and where did language cause problems?

### 2.10 Local buyers
1. Walk me through the **last** property you bought or seriously pursued.
2. How did you find it, and how did you check it was legitimate?
3. Who did you trust to help, and why?
4. What did the process cost beyond the price (fees, professionals, time)?
5. What information about the property, seller or procedure was missing?
6. What went wrong or was slower than expected?
7. What did you already pay for to make it easier?

### 2.11 Banks
1. Walk me through the **last** property-related loan/mortgage or valuation your team handled.
2. Where do you most often find missing or unreliable documentation on the property or borrower?
3. What verification of the property/seller/professional do you require, and how do you get it today?
4. What causes the most delay or decline in these cases?
5. How do you currently confirm a property's registration/title status?
6. What information, if reliably available up front, would speed your decisions?
7. How do foreign/diaspora applicants differ operationally?

### 2.12 Lawyers
1. Walk me through the **last** property transaction you advised on.
2. Where did due diligence take the most time, and why?
3. What documents/records were hardest to obtain or verify?
4. What do clients typically pay you for in these matters? (Ranges/what drives the fee.)
5. What do clients most often misunderstand about ownership, tax (cITI/cIPI) or the deed/registration process?
6. What errors by other parties (agents, sellers) created risk or rework?
7. How do foreign buyers' matters differ?

### 2.13 Notaries
1. Walk me through the **last** public deed for a property transfer you executed.
2. What documentation problems most often delay signing?
3. Where do parties arrive unprepared, and what do they misunderstand?
4. How does coordination with the Conservatória/registration and the tax position work in practice?
5. What causes appointments to be rescheduled or deeds to be held up?
6. What information, if standardised and reliable beforehand, would reduce friction at signing?
7. How do foreign/diaspora parties change the process (powers of attorney, translation, remote steps)?

### 2.14 Building-materials suppliers
1. Walk me through your **last** significant sale to a builder or homeowner.
2. How did that customer find you, and how do most customers find you?
3. Do you advertise or pay for leads/visibility? How much?
4. What information do customers most often lack (availability, price, specs, delivery)?
5. Where do you lose sales — stockouts, price opacity, trust, logistics?
6. How do you decide whether to extend credit/terms to a new contractor customer?
7. What would make it easier for the right customers to find and trust you?

## 4. Scoring model (compare interview results)

Score each interview on **five dimensions, 1–5**. Definitions are behavioural (based on what happened/what they pay), not attitudinal.

| Dimension | 1 (low) | 3 (medium) | 5 (high) |
|-----------|---------|------------|----------|
| **Problem severity** | Minor annoyance; no real cost | Real time/money lost occasionally | Deals lost / major cost / blocking |
| **Frequency** | Rare (once ever) | A few times a year | Constant / every transaction |
| **Willingness/ability to pay** | Never pays for help | Sometimes pays / small amounts | Already pays real money for a workaround |
| **Reachability** | Hard to reach / offline | Reachable via some channel | Active on WhatsApp/online, easy to onboard |
| **Urgency** | Can live with it indefinitely | Wants it fixed eventually | Actively seeking a fix now |

**Composite scoring**

- **Total score** = sum of five dimensions (5–25).
- **Weighted priority score (RECOMMENDATION):**
  `Priority = 0.30·Severity + 0.25·Frequency + 0.25·WTP + 0.10·Reach + 0.10·Urgency` (weights to tune).
- **Interpretation (HYPOTHESIS — tune with data):**
  - **≥ 4.0 weighted (or ≥ 20/25):** strong, monetisable pain → prioritise for MVP.
  - **3.0–3.9 (14–19):** real but secondary → SHOULD/COULD.
  - **< 3.0 (< 14):** weak signal → deprioritise or reframe.

High severity + frequency there signals administrative-relief value (feeds the government pitch), while WTP is read as institutional willingness to collaborate/fund, not consumer WTP.

## 5. Targets & cadence (RECOMMENDATION)

- **Coverage:** aim for a minimum viable sample per priority segment (agents, contractors, tradespeople, foreign buyers, landowners first), plus at least a few interviews each with municipalities, INGT, notaries and lawyers.
- **Cadence:** run in waves; re-score and re-rank after each wave; stop a segment when answers stop surprising (saturation).
- **Output:** a ranked pain/segment table feeding (a) MVP prioritisation, (b) the investor WTP story, (c) the government administrative-relief story, and (d) the open-questions register (`25-open-questions.md`).

---

*Guides are behavioural by design. If an interviewer finds themselves describing Kavíla or asking "would you use it", stop — return to the last real event.*
